Learning Outcomes from Week 11

Graphics and Multimedia

  • These are visuals, sounds, motion, everything that makes a website feel alive.
  • They’re not just decoration. They help explain, engage, and literally bring the page to life.

Computer Graphics

  • Computer graphics are pictures or videos made with computers. They changed how we make movies, games, ads, and digital art.
  • There are two main types:
    1. Raster: These are made of tiny dots called pixels. They’re mostly great unless you zoom in. (Example of a raster tool I’ve used: Adobe Photoshop)
    2. Vector: Made of curves and math-based lines. They never lose quality even if you zoom in. (Example of a raster tool I’ve used: Adobe Illustrator)
  • Raster editors (like Photoshop, GIMP, Paint.NET) work with pixels.
  • Vector editors (like Illustrator, CorelDRAW, Inkscape) work with lines and shapes.
  • So basically, raster = photos, vector = drawings.

Adding Images with HTML

You use the img tag to show pictures. -Example: img src="example.jpg" alt="description of image" - all this being inside <>
  • src = where the image is stored
  • alt = text that shows if the image can’t load (and helps with accessibility).
  • Keep image files in your “images” folder, name them clearly, and always use smaller file sizes so your page loads fast.

Multimedia

  • This is the combo of all types of media like text, sound, video, animation, interactivity.
  • It can be linear (pre-determines for the user) or non-linear (you control it, like games or interactive lessons).
  • Also can be live or recorded.
  • Multimedia shows up in almost every field like education, business, entertainment, ads, medicine, even research.
  • It’s also getting more advanced with haptic tech (feeling vibrations/touch) and new ideas like smell/taste simulations which is really cool.

HTML for Audio and Video:

  • Supported formats:
    1. Audio: MP3, WAV, AAC, OGG, FLAC
    2. Video: MP4, WebM, OGG
  • Streaming means you can watch or listen while it’s loading (like YouTube).
  • Downloading means waiting until it’s done.
