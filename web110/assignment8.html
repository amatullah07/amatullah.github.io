Learning Outcome From Unit 08

Coincidentally, I had to watch Personal Information Management (PIM) videos and Digital Security videos at my job this week. So watching and reading this Unit felt nice as I already knew some stuff from in here. Below are paragraphs I wrote along when I did the reading and watching the videos. I also made bullet points in the end that will help me when I turn back to these notes.

The readings made the threats feel real: phishing, ransomware, spyware: basically anything that sneaks in when you click dumb links or overshare PII. It broke down the scary words into real-life moments (like “that sketchy email” or “odd download”), which actually made me care about the tiny habits that protect you. Usually, I allow all cookies and don’t really care about visiting fishy websites, maybe not anymore though.

On the protection side, the basics are huge: HTTPS/encryption, multi-factor authentication, password managers, firewalls, and keeping software updated. Tech helps, but the readings kept reminding me that user choices matter most and that one bad click undoes a lot. The ethics stuff also stood out: IT pros must protect data and respect privacy, which really highlighted responsibility.

The student presentation looked really clean and useful. I took notes on layout, simple visuals, and quick demos I want to copy for my final project. I actually changed my Chrome privacy settings after the lesson which felt productive. Funny moment: I tried the EICAR antivirus test file and got a 404 that was kinda phishy-looking itself, so the whole thing reminded me to always double-check test sources. I’m not sure if it was, but the moment was pretty funny for me. 😅

Exam prep Bullet points:

  1. Common threats: phishing, viruses, worms, Trojans, ransomware, spyware, botnets.
  2. Defense basics: HTTPS/SSL, encryption, MFA, password managers, antivirus, firewalls, updates.
  3. User habits matter: check URLs, don’t reuse passwords, don’t click weird links.
  4. Ethics: handle PII carefully; IT pros have a duty of care.
  5. Terms that will remind me of this Unit: PII, phishing, malware, ransomware, encryption, SSL/TLS, MFA, firewall, EICAR.
